Hexamusic

Hexamusic is a touch interactive electronic instrument based on Neo-Riemannian theory. Users can play music through direct touch, proximity sensing, and multi-touch. The main body of Hexamusic is a regular hexagonal prism made of cardboard. There are 7 touch sensing areas on the top and 6 proximity detection areas on the side. The top 7 touch sensing areas correspond to 7 tones in a hexagonal unit in the Riemannian Tonnetz, and the corresponding chords can be played by touching 3 touch areas simultaneously. In addition, touching two adjacent touch sensing areas at the same time can play the corresponding five-tones of ancient China, namely, Gong, Shang, Jue, Zhi, and Yu, providing more convenience for Chinese traditional music such as "Jasmine", "Spring River Flower Moon Night", "Mountain Flow". Among the 6 sides, we used proximity capacitive touch sensing technology to detect the user's proximity to achieve the effect of proximity interaction. When the palm of the hand of users approaches to the sides, Hexamusic plays a melody composed of the corresponding chords on the top to increase the playability of the instrument.
